LIBERATION FLEET PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: July 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Liberation Fleet ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Liberation Fleet application and related services (the "Service").

By creating an account or using the Service, you agree to this Privacy Policy and our Community Standards. If you do not agree, do not use the Service.

IMPORTANT: Liberation Fleet is a mutual-aid coordination tool for adults 18 years of age or older. We are not a bank, credit union, money transmitter, or payment processor. Gifts are recorded and coordinated through the Service, but payments occur on third-party platforms (such as PayPal, Venmo, Cash App, or Zelle) outside of our control.


1. WHO THIS POLICY APPLIES TO

This policy applies to anyone who registers for an account, joins a crew, or otherwise uses the Service.


2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

2.1 Account and profile information
When you sign up, we collect:
- Username
- Email address
- Password (stored only as a secure one-way hash; we do not store your plain-text password)

You may also provide profile and mutual-aid information, including:
- Whether you are in need of aid
- Emergency level and survival-aid preferences
- Payment platform handles you choose to share with your crew (for example, a Venmo username)
- Other profile settings you enter in the app

2.2 Crew and membership information
If you create or join a crew, we collect:
- Crew name, size limits, privacy setting (public or private), and scope (online or local)
- Join codes
- Optional location information such as zip code and search radius for local crews
- Your role and status in the crew (organizer, honorary member, season participation, estimated monthly contribution, priority scores, and related season data)

2.3 Gift and mutual-aid records
To operate gift logging, verification, reception order, survival thresholds, and seasonal cycles, we store gift and mutual-aid data on our servers, including:
- Who gave a gift, who received it, and any middleman involved (by user account)
- Gift amounts
- Whether a gift was direct, initiated through a middleman, or completed by a middleman
- Whether a gift applied to a survival threshold or a cycle
- Payment platform used
- Gift verification status and timestamps
- Aggregated reception and contribution totals used for mutual-aid calculations

This structural gift and mutual-aid information is stored on our servers in readable form so the Service can calculate reception order, contributions, and season rules. It is not hidden from our systems.

2.4 Encrypted content (end-to-end encryption)
We also support encryption for certain user-generated content, including crew proposals, forums, projects, and gift log display fields. For gift log entries, this may include encrypted versions of:
- Display names shown in the log
- The human-readable gift message text
- Payment platform name as shown in the log

We store:
- Your public encryption key
- An encrypted backup of your private encryption key (wrapped with your 12-word recovery key; we cannot read your private key without it)
- Crew encryption keys wrapped for each crew member
- Encrypted content envelopes (ciphertext only)

Your login password is used only for authentication. It is not used to encrypt your private key backup on new accounts. Resetting your login password does not change or recover your recovery key.

We do not hold the keys needed to decrypt crew content. Only you and other members of your crew who have valid keys can decrypt that content on their devices.

Encryption does not currently extend to gift amounts, participant identities stored in gift records, or mutual-aid calculation data described in section 2.3.

2.5 Technical and usage information
We automatically collect certain technical information needed to operate the Service, such as:
- Authentication tokens issued when you sign in
- Server logs (for example, IP address, browser or device type, request timestamps, and error logs)
- Information required to maintain security and prevent abuse

2.6 Communications and account recovery
If you use password reset or other account recovery features, we process your email address and temporary reset tokens to complete those requests. Password reset changes your login credentials only. It does not restore access to encrypted content; you still need your recovery key for that.

2.7 Safety reports (report packets)
When you use Report on content or a profile, we collect:
- Your user id (as reporter)
- Report reason category and optional short note
- Target content type and identifiers (for example message, post, comment, or profile ids)
- Suspected user ids you identify
- A one-time evidence snapshot: decrypted text (truncated) and, when you include them, limited media metadata or bytes you choose to attach for that report
- Timestamps and status of the report (received, escalated, closed)

Routine end-to-end encrypted content remains unread by us. Report evidence is encrypted at rest with a separate report-evidence key (not your crew or fleet content key). Access is limited to safety operations, contracted moderation vendors (when used), and disclosures required by law — including CyberTipline reports to NCMEC for apparent child sexual exploitation when we have actual knowledge.

Retention: non-CSAM report packets are kept for a limited safety window (typically up to 90 days unless an investigation is open). CSAM-related or lawfully escalated packets are preserved as required for CyberTipline / legal compliance.


3. HOW WE USE INFORMATION

We use the information we collect to:
- Create and manage your account
- Authenticate you and maintain your session
- Enable you to create, join, and participate in crews
- Record gifts and run mutual-aid features (reception order, thresholds, cycles, verification workflows, and season management)
- Display information to you and to other members of your crew as the app is designed to do
- Operate encryption key exchange and store encrypted content on your behalf
- Process safety reports, quarantine abusive content identifiers, freeze abusive accounts, and file legally required reports (including to NCMEC)
- Respond to support requests and secure the Service
- Comply with applicable law and enforce our Community Standards

We do not sell your personal information.


4. WHO CAN SEE YOUR INFORMATION

4.1 Within your crew
Much of the information in the Service is shared with other members of crews you join, including usernames, payment handles you provide, gift activity, and mutual-aid status relevant to your crew's season.

4.2 Crew discovery
If a crew is set to "Public," certain crew details (such as name, size, and scope) may be visible to other users searching for crews. Private crews are not listed in public search results.

4.3 Service operators
Authorized administrators and hosting providers may access server-stored data as needed to operate, secure, and maintain the Service. Because much gift and mutual-aid data is stored in readable form on our servers, it could be accessed by someone with database or administrative access.

4.4 Legal and safety
We may disclose information if we believe it is reasonably necessary to comply with law, respond to lawful requests, protect the rights and safety of users or the public, or investigate fraud or security issues.


5. HOW WE PROTECT INFORMATION

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect information, including:
- Password hashing for account credentials
- HTTPS encryption for data in transit between your browser and our servers
- Access controls on our systems
- Client-side encryption for certain display content, as described in section 2.4

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. A security incident could expose information stored on our servers, including gift amounts and who gave to whom, even when some display text is encrypted.


6. WHAT WE DO NOT COLLECT OR PROCESS

- We do not process card payments or hold your money.
- We do not receive full payment credentials from PayPal, Venmo, or similar services through normal use of the app.
- We are not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party payment platforms you use to send or receive money.


7. DATA RETENTION

We retain information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. You may request account deletion by contacting us; some information may be retained where required by law or for legitimate business purposes (such as security logs).


8. YOUR CHOICES

- You can update certain profile and payment information in the app.
- You can leave a crew or stop using the Service.
- Encrypted content is unlocked with your 12-word recovery key on each device (or for the browser session if you choose to remember it). Your login password does not decrypt crew content on new accounts.
- If you lose your recovery key, we cannot restore access to encrypted content.
- You may decline to accept this policy, but you will not be able to register for an account.


9. CHILDREN AND AGE REQUIREMENT

The Service is intended only for adults 18 years of age or older. We do not knowingly allow minors to register. If you believe a minor has created an account, Report it and contact us so we can disable the account.


10. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will revise the "Last updated" date when we do. Continued use of the Service after changes become effective means you accept the updated policy.


11. CONTACT

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, contact the Liberation Fleet project administrators through the contact method provided in the app or on the project website.


SUMMARY (NOT A SUBSTITUTE FOR THE FULL POLICY)

- We store account, crew, gift, and mutual-aid data on our servers.
- Gift amounts and who gave to whom are stored in readable form for app features to work.
- Some gift log display text may be encrypted so we cannot read it.
- Crew members can see information relevant to your shared mutual-aid activity.
- We are not a bank or payment processor; payments happen on external platforms.
